Here are some interesting facts about India's history:

*   **Ancient Civilization:** The Indus Valley Civilization, one of the world's earliest, flourished in the Indian subcontinent around 2500-1900 BCE.
*   **Cradle of Religions:** Hinduism, Buddhism, Jainism, and Sikhism all originated in India. These religions are followed by 25% of the world's population.
*   **A Center for learning**: The world's first university was established in Takshila in 700 BC.
*   **Mathematics Innovation**: Algebra, Trigonometry, and Calculus originated in India, and the decimal system was developed there in 100 B.C.
*   **The Maurya Empire (322–185 BCE)** unified most of the Indian subcontinent into one state and was the largest empire ever to exist on the Indian subcontinent.
*   **Golden Age:** The Gupta Empire (4th to 6th centuries CE) saw a resurgence of Hindu religious and intellectual life, known as the "Classical" or "Golden Age of India."
*   **Largest Democracy:** Since its independence in 1947, India has been the world's most populous democracy.
*   **Never Invaded:** India has a long history of peace, with no invasions of other countries in the last 100,000 years.
